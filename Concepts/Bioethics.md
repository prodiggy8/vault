## Cloning

### What is it?

Clones are exact genetic copies. It can happen naturally (twins) or made artificially in two ways:

- **Artificial Embryo Twinning:** artificially reproduce natural identical twins.
- **Somatic Nuclear Transfer:** isolates somatic cells from adult, remove nucleus and all of its DNA. Then, transfer to an egg cell.

Cloning an individual is not the same as cloning a single gene.

### Why do it?

- Cloning genetically engineered animals to study diseases.
- Cloning to make stem cells.
- Reviving endangered or extinct species (requires perfectly preserved DNA and closely related species to be surrogate).
- Reproducing diseased pet.
- Cloning livestock that produces high quality milk, meat, or even drugs.
### Myths

- Not instant
- Not carbon copies (randomness)
- Can happen naturally (twins)
- It’s a very old technology
- Cloned animals *do not* live short lives or suffer from health problems

### Why not do it?

- Although safe for animals, no data for humans.
- Although when done right it can cause no problems, it can also cause even then; failure rate is high.
- Loss of uniqueness and will be “shadow” of donor
- Designer clones
