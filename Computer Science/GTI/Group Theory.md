A group $G$ is a pair $(S, \cdot)$ where $S$ is a set and $\cdot$ is a binary operation $S \times S \rightarrow S$ such that:
1. **Closure:** $a \cdot b \in S$
2. **Associativity:** $(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)$
3. **Identity:** $e \cdot a = a \cdot e = a$
4. **Inverse:** $a \cdot b = b \cdot a = e$

$G$ is commutative if it also satisfies: $a \cdot b = b \cdot a$

$g \in S$ is a generator if every element in $S$ can be derived as $g \cdot g \dots g$ some $\#$ of times
A group $G$ is cyclic if it has a generator

$(\mathbb{Z}_n, +_n)=\{a^i \mid i \ge 1\}$ with $gcd(a, n)=1$
$(\mathbb{Z}_p^*, *_p)$ is cyclic if $p$ is prime

Order of $a$ is the smallest $n$ such that $a^n=e$

Order of $(\mathbb{Z}_p^*, *_p)$ is $\phi(n)$
Order of any $x$ is at most $\phi(n)$ by Euler's Theorem

**Subgroups**

**Lagrange's Theorem:** If $G$ is a finite group and $H$ is a subgroup of $G$, then the order of $H$ divides the order of $G$
**Corollary:** if $x \in G$, then order of $x$ divides order of $G$
**Corollary:** if $x \in G$, then $x^{|G|}=e$


**Lemma:** If $x \in G$, then $X=\{x^k \mid k \ge 1\}$ is a cyclic subgroup of $G$ generated by $x$

**Theorem:** a group of prime order is cyclic and every non-identity is a generator 

**Isomorphism**

$(G, \cdot)$ and $(H, \triangle)$ are groups with $f: G \rightarrow H$ so that $f(x \cdot y) = f(x) \triangle f(y)$

**Theorem:** If $f: G \rightarrow H$ is an isomorphism then $f(e_G)=e_H$ and also $f(x)^{-1}=f(x^{-1})$

Every cyclic group $G$ of order $n$ is isomorphic to $(\mathbb{Z_n}, +_n)$ using $f(x^k)=k$
Every group of order n is isomorphic to a subgroup of the permutation group $S$ using $\lambda_x(g)=xg$

**Rings**

A ring $R$ is a set together with two binary operation $+$ and $\times$ satisfying:
1. $(R, +)$ is a commutative group
2. $\times$ is associative
3. $\times$ distributes over $+$, that is, $(a + b) \times c = (a \times c) + (b \times c)$

**Fields**

A field $F$ is a set together with two binary operations $+$ and $\times$ satisfying:
1.  $(F, +)$ is a commutative group
2.  $(F \setminus \{0\}, \times)$ is a commutative group
3. $\times$ distributes over $+$
