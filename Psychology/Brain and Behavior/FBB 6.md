**ADHD**
- Inattention (at least 6 symptoms for 6 months): careless mistakes, difficulty sustaining attention, does listen when spoken to, lack of organization, avoids tasks with sustained mental effort.

- Hyperactivity (at least 6 symptoms for 6 months): fidgets, leaves seat, restlessness, talks too much

**Cognitive enhancement:** Adderall, Ritalin help and are harmless

**Blood-brain barrier**: some sites are barrier-free such as pituitary and pineal glands, drugs target these.

How body eliminates drugs?

- Broken down in kidneys, liver, intestine
- Some can't be removed and accumulate, becoming toxic (lead)

**Agonist:** enhances function of synapse
**Antagonist:** blocks or decreases function of synapse

They can target **any stage of the transmitter lifecycle**

Example: ACh
- Choline-rich diet, nicotine, black widow spider venon are agonists
- Botulin toxin, curare are antagonists

**Decreased response to drug due to repeated usage**
- Metabolic tolerance (increase of enzymes to catabolize)
- Cellular tolerance (brain activity adapts)
- Learned tolerance (people learn to cope)

Prisoner's given alcohol: after a few weeks both blood level and signs of intoxication fell

**Sensitization** happens with intermittent use (increased responsiveness)

It causes structural changes, hence it's long lasting
