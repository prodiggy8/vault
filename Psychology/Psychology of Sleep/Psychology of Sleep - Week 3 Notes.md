Theory: set of statements that describes general principles about how variables relate to one another

Hypothesis: predictions about the outcomes of your research based on theory

Data: set of observations

Strange situation: mom leaves kid with stranger and then comes back
Kid's behavior is recorded

Some follow the kid for various years, longitudinal studies

Research design: postulates hypothesis, then conduct study and data either support or leads to revision in theory

Conceptual variable to operational definition (child's relation with caregiver vs how does the child reacts when the mother leaves)

Variables must have 2 levels (or states)
Constants have one
Measured variable: whose levels are observed and recorded
Manipulated variable: research controls
Construct: a variable of interest, starts abstract and then becomes formally defined
	Conceptual variable: abstract
	Operational variable: defined in a way that can be measured

Goal: claim causation

Frequency claim: one variable
Association claim: two variables, related
Causal claim: two variables, one which causes the other

**Validities**

**Construct validity:** how well the variables are measured or manipulated. The extent to which the **operational variables approximate conceptual variables.**
**External validity:** Extend to which a study generalize to some larger population as well to other times or situations (W.E.I.R.D)
**Statistical validity:** extent to which data support conclusions. Strength of correlation and statistical significance.
**Internal validity:** the relationship between one variable and another, the extent to which A, rather than a confounding variable C, is responsible for changes in C
