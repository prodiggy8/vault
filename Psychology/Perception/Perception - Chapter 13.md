*Kinesthesia* is the sense of movement  (dynamic) and motion of the body. 

*Proprioception* is the (static) sense of position of body parts.

*Somatosensation* encompasses sensory signals from skin, muscles, tendons, joints, and internal receptors.

### Touch Receptors

Internal resistance: how hard is to flow?
Membrane resistance: how hard is to leak out?

Myelin is a fatty, insulating layer around axon.
- Increase membrane resistance, ions can't leak out of the wrapper section
- Decreases membrane capacitance, less charge gets "stored" in the membrane
- Spaced by gaps called *nodes of Ranvier* full of sodium channels that regenerate signal

Thicker axons have lower internal resistance, so current spreads faster.
Myelin does what's described above.

**A-alpha fibers:** wide-diameter, myelinated fibers that transmit signals from *proprioceptive receptors* in muscles and tendons.

**A-beta fibers:** wide-diameter, myelinated fibers that transmit signals from *mechanical stimulation*.

**A-delta fibers:** intermediate-sized, myelinated fibers that transmit *pain* and *temperature*.

**C fibers:** narrow, unmyelinated fibers that transmit *pain* and *temperature*.

#### Tactile Receptors

- Epidermis and dermis
- Can be categorized by:
	- Type of stimulation it responds to
	- Size of receptive field
	- Rate of adaptation

##### Touch

Mechanoreceptors: respond to mechanical stimuli (pressure, vibration, or movement)

- **Meissner corpuscles:** fast adaptation, small receptive field (FA I)
- **Merkel discs:** slow adaptation, small receptive field (SA I)
- **Pacinian corpuscles:** fast adaptation, large receptive field (FA II)
- **Ruffini endings:** slow adaptation, large receptive field (SA II)

Small receptive fields gives more acuity.
Larger gives more coverage.

*Adaptation:* how fast the neurons stop firing giving continued stimuli. Slow keeps firing as long as the stimulus is there.

**SA I** for *texture and pattern*
	Merkel
	Fibers TERMINATE in endings
	
**SA II** measures *skin stretch, finger position*
	Ruffini
	
**FA I** measures object slip, edges, and fine features, grasp stability, *low-frequency vibration*
	Meissner
	
**FA II** measures *high-frequency vibrations*, fine features
	Pacinian


SA1 and FA1 are analogous to CONES AND RODS -> one gives acuity and other gives sensitivity to low-intensity stimulation, **respectively**.

**Kinesthetic receptors**
- Muscle spindle: senses *muscle tension*, which are flexed and which are relaxed? *Angle between joints as well.*

*Ian Waterman*: mechanoreceptors destroyed by viral infection, can only locate himself in space via vision.

##### Thermoreceptors

- Signal information about temperature CHANGE
	- Only outside the range of 30 and 36 degrees (normal skin temperature)
- **30 cold fibers for every warmth fiber.**
- C-fibers (warm) and A-delta fibers (cold). This is only valid for NON PAINFUL temperature. 
- They get mixed for painful temperature (sharp vs throbbing pain, both activate)

##### Nociceptors

Like thermoception, two groups: A-delta and C fibers.

Painful events have two stages:
- quick sharp pain (A-delta)
- followed by a throbbing sensation (C fibers)

How do they detect chemical and thermal stimulation that produces pain?
- thermoTRP ion channels, *regulate flow of atoms and molecules across the membrane of a cell*
- Pepper and wasabi cause pain. Feels hot because activate thermoTRP which also responds to intense heat.
- Mint feels cold because activate TRP that responds to cold.


#### Pleasant Touch

C-fiber type CT afferents
- CT afferents are not related to pain or itch
- Only in hairy skin
- Slowly moving lightly applied forces
- processed in frontal cortex instead of S1 or S2



## Pathway!

- Similar to other senses, receptors are combined in nerve trunks. However there are various of them, from hands, arms, legs, etc! Messages also go much farther, up to 2 meters!

Information moves up the *spinal chord*.

Labeled lines: each fiber type codes for a particular sensation. The lines become interconnected in the spinal chord. They enter the spinal chord through the **dorsal horn**.

The dorsal horn is organized in multiple layers (laminae). Somatotopically organized (adjacent areas map to adjacent points of the horn). Sensations get enriched there.

From there, two major pathways:
1. **Spinothalamic Pathway:** slower; carries most of pain and temperature information.
		Crosses in the horn
2. **DCML Pathway:** A$\alpha$ and A$\beta$, faster; tactile and kinesthetic information. Involved in planning and executing rapid movements.
		Crosses only in thalamus


Both go to **ventral posterior nucleus** of the thalamus. Then to **S1**, located in the parietal lobe.
Resembles ON-center RGCs.

Then **S2**. The motor areas are located just in front of it.
To some extent, S2 is somatotopically organized.

**Homunculus:** lots of space for face, tongue, teeth/gum/jaw, intra-abdominal organs, and fingers.


*Pleasant touch* follows a different trajectory.
- CT goes to insula, which regulates body and linking sensory to emotional systems.
- Orbitofrontal cortex also involved.
- Both related to taste as well!

Pain top-down process:
	Responses to noxious stimuli can be moderated by anticipation, religious belief, prior experience, watching others respond, money, and excitement and more. Example: Wounded soldier in battle who does not feel pain until after battle.

Pain involves emotional response -> Pre-frontal cortex involved (insula, orbitofrontal cortex)

**Phantom limb:** Sensation perceived from a physically amputated limb of the body.
Parts of brain listening to missing limbs not fully aware of altered connections, so they attribute activity in these areas to stimulation from missing limb.
Body image is inaccurate.

e.g., can feel missing hand from areas in arm and face, because these areas are close in S1.

Pascual-Leone & Hamilton (2001) blindfolded participants for 5 days.
Tested their sensitivity to braille stimulation on their right index finger each day, scanned their brains using fMRI.
First day, only area S1 in left hemisphere activated.
Comprehension: Why left hemisphere?
By fifth day, S1 activation had decreased and V1 activation increased!
V1 switched over from vision to analyzing touch sensations.
Once blindfolds removed, neural functioning changed back to normal.
