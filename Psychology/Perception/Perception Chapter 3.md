**Remarks:**
- **Left visual field** maps to **right LGN** 
- **Right visual field** maps to **left LGN** 
- Each layer **gets information from one eye, alternated**
- Magnocellular connects with M-cells and in turn to rods
- Parvocellular connects with P-cells and in turn to cones

Poltoratksi et al., showed separate inputs to each eye; they are perceived as one image.

LGN is a hub to access (and send!) visual information, not only striate cortex.
When we sleep, access is limited, so even if eyes are opened, we will see nothing.
##### Striate Cortex (V1)

- Stripe receptive fields
- Six layers, LGN goes to 4 ($4C\alpha$ for magnocellular and $4C\beta$ for parvocellular)
- 100x times more neurons than LGN (**cortical magnification** of fovea by eccentricity)
- Right visual field go to left side of cortex and vice-versa

**Visual crowding:** clutter on peripheral object recognition; _objects that can be easily identified in insolation seem indistinct when surrounded by other objects_.

Hubel and Wiesel's experiment on cats: **orientation** of V1 cells.
- Ideal orientation; cells respond to lines with at most **30 degrees** difference from it.
- **Simple cells** respond to bars of light
- **Complex cells** respond to both bars of light and dark

- Cells can respond to **both eyes**, but **prefer one**
- Some cells also prefer **bars of a certain length**

The cells are organized in:
	**ocular dominance columns** (left vs. right eye preferred)
	**orientation columns**

A hyper column is a column composed of two ocular dominance columns from both eyes, and all orientations.

**Selective adaptation:** tiring the cells for an orientation so different ones respond.
Same happens for spatial frequency.

Blobs are present regularly, we think they process color.

#### Why spatial frequencies?
==Different spatial frequencies mean different types of information (fine detail in low vs. broad outline in high).==


**Development of Vision**
- Forced-choice preferential-looking paradigm
- Cones taking final shape and RGCs migrating until **4 years old**
- Young children are **not very sensitive to high spatial frequencies (blurry)**
- Children choose complex pattern!

**Strabismus:** misalignment of two eyes (fovea of one eye and non-fovea on the other)
**Cataract:** clouding of lens of the eye

If left untreated until 5 years of age the cortical effect is permanent.
