
**FOBB Functional Neuroanatomy Study Guide**

This part of the exam involves learning the basic neurological structures **and their functions** that were covered in this course.

You may be asked to draw and label these structures on your own, or to identify them on diagrams provided. In addition to labeling, you need to know what they do in a basic, overarching sense (hence “functional neuroanatomy”). This portion of the exam will only include what is listed here.

2. **Primary Visual Cortex**
Located in the occipital lobe. Divided in V1, V2, and so on. V1 focuses on recognizing lines. V2 focuses on recognizing lines in movement, color, and shape. It divides into the "what" (ventral) and the "how" (dorsal) pathway for higher level understanding.

3. Primary Auditory Cortex
Similarly divided in A1, A2, etc.

4. Primary Somatosensory Cortex
5. Broca’s Area
Responsible for articulating speech, located in the right side of the temporal lobe.

6. Wernicke’s Area
Responsible for understanding speech, located in the left side of the temporal lobe.

7. Auditory cortices and their asymmetry in the left and right hemisphere and the functionality associated with this
Left focuses on language (Wernicke's is there!) and right focuses on music.
