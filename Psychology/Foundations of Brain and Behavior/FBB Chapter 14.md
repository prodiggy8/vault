**Learning and Remembering**

**Learning:** change in behavior as a result of experience
**Memory:** ability to recall or recognize previous experience

Memory trace:
- Mental representation of previous experience
- Correspond to a **physical change** in the synapse in neurons, called an **Engram**

Experiment: rats in water, memorize location of platform based on objects outside

**Pavlovian conditioning**: 
	Neural stimulus such as a tone (CS) comes to **elicit a response** (CR) **because of its repeated pairing** with some event such as delivery of food (UCS)

**Operant Conditioning**
	Consequences of particular behavior increase or decrease the probability of the behavior occurring again.

**Implicit memory:** unconscious, demonstrate knowledge but cannot explicitly retrieve information
**Explicit memory:** can retrieve

**Priming:** ==implicit==, automatic association of memory
Seeing words related to falling or autumn will get people to give different definitions of "fall"

**Amnesia causes inability to access explicit memory**
Will say "autumn" or "falling" but does not remember the list of words

**Differences in memory**
Short-term (few minutes)
Long-term
- **Explicit** (conscious)
	- **Episodic** (events that happened to you)
	- **Semantic** (general knowledge)
- **Implicit** (unconscious)
	- Priming
	- **Procedural** (motor)

Implicit is processed **bottom-up**, encoding is passive
Explicit is processed **top-down**, subject **reorganizes information before it is encoded**
	Can be inaccurate, changes over time

**No single place can be identified as the location of memory or learning!**

Information from different sensory modalities (vision, audition) is processed and stored in different places
