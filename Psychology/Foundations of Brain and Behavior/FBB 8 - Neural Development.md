Embryo's brain: same across species

- Neural tube

**Neural tube** has the stem cells that give birth to neurons.
It corresponds to **subventricular zone** in adults

Stem cells -> progenitor cells -> neuroblast or glioblast -> neurons or glial cells -> differentiation

Neural tube defects:
- Anencephaly
- **Craniorachischisis:** completely open
- **Encephalocele:** hernia in meninges

1. **Cell birth: neurogenesis or gliogenesis**
	- Most of it done by birth
	- Exception: hippocampus
	- Defects:
		- Microcephaly (Zika)
		- Macrocephaly (genetic)
2. **Migration**
	- Radial glial cells guide the way
	- Defects:
		- Poorly formed gyri or sulci
		- No corpus callosum
		- Autism
3. **Differentiation**
4. **Maturation**
	- Dendrite (arborization) and axon growth
	- **Growth cones** extend towards target
	- **Other branches follow filopodia when it finds a target**
	- Axon growth led by cell adhesion molecule and tropic molecules.
	- Defects:
		- Autism: less white matter
5. Synaptogenesis
	- Many more than needed
	- Pruning and cell death happens
		- Darwinism
		- Apoptosis (genetically programmed)
	- Until 30s, 42% cells die, **experience dependent** process
6. Myelination
	- Continues through life
	- Simpler areas finish first
	- Cortex is last
	- Frontal lobe is sensitive to **epigenetics**
		- ACE have huge impact
	- Reduction in thickness & increased connectivity (default network)
	- Change in cortex correlates with intelligence
