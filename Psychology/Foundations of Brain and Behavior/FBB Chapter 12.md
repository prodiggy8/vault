Emotions

Experiment with painful hot stimuli and photos of ex-partner shows emotional pain **hurts the same way as physical**. Same pills reduce both pains.

**Emotions:** cognitive interpretations of subjective feelings
**Motivation:** behavior that seems purposeful and goal oriented

- Inferred, subjective
- Can occur without awareness or intent
- **Regulatory behaviors** for survival (eating) vs. **non-regulatory** (curiosity)

Three components of emotions:
1. **Autonomic response** (increased heart rate)
	Hypothalamus
2. **Subjective feelings** (feat)
	Amygdala
3. **Cognitions** (thoughts about the experience)
	Cortex

**Theories**
1. Constructive
**Brain interprets physiological changes as emotions**
Can't be true because same ANS response can lead to different emotions

2. Appraisal
Emotions arise from **cognitive interpretation (appraisal) of a situation**

3. Neuropsychological
Lesion in **right hemisphere**: emotional indifference    ==> **interpreting emotions**  
Lesion in **left hemisphere**: fear and depression           ==> **producing emotions**

**Limbic System**
Adjacent to neocortex (right below prefrontal cortex)
Encircle the brainstem
Contains cingulate gyrus, hippocampal formation, amygdala, mammillothalamic tract, and anterior thalamus
**Neural basis of emotion**

**Amygdala:**
Almond-shaped
Receives input from **all** sensory systems
Sends projection to **hypothalamus** (influences autonomic, hormonal response) and **brainstem**
Connection to prefrontal cortex, **influences cognitive awareness of positive and negative consequences**

Removal of amygdala (Kluver-Bucy): loss of fear, indiscriminate dietary behavior, atypical sexual behavior, react to every visual stimulus.

**Prefrontal cortex:** specifies goals of movement, creates conscious emotional states produced by limbic system

If damaged:
- can't express emotions or recognize in others
- apathy and loss of initiative
- inability to plan or organize
