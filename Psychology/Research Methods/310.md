**Measurement**

**Inference validity: two factors**
Internal validity: if study is able to determine causation!
	How to measure: ceteris paribus
External validity:
	Generalization to overall population

**Construct validity:**
a. **Translation validity** (i.e., operationalization)
If the operational variable approaches the conceptual variable.
Two forms of translation validity.
- Face validity:
	It looks like the conceptual variable
	Weakest of the two
- Content validity
	Contains all the parts that theory says it should have
	E.g. Krumrei says intellectual humility has 4 factors (respect to others, etc...)
b. **Criterion validity**
- Predictive: a measure's ability to predict future behavior
- Concurrent: correlation of a measure with a similar established measure (same construct)
	- WAIS vs. Stamford-Binet correlated
- Convergent: measure's association with measures of a **similar construct**
	- Intelligence vs. academic performance correlated
- Discriminant: A measure's weak association with measures of unrelated constructs


Reliability: overall consistency of a measure
1. Test-retest
2. Inter-rater reliability (2+ observers reports are consistent; developmental studies)
	1. Use of intraclass correlation ($\kappa$)
	2. Acceptable is **.8 or better**
	3. **Calculate Pearson's for every pair and average**
3. Internal consistency (across items; psychometrics studies)
	1. Use of Cronbach's alpha ($\alpha$)
	2. Acceptable is **.7 or better**
	3. **Calculate Pearson's for every pair and average**
