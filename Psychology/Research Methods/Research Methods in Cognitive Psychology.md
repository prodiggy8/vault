##### 1. Scientific Understanding of Behavior

Research principles: empiricism, skepticism, alternative explanations, covariation of cause and effect.

Belmont report: ethical principles for human subjects
- respect for persons, their choices, autonomy, informed consent
- beneficence, maximizing benefits, balancing risks and benefits
- justice, fair distribution of benefit and risks, avoids exploitation

APA Ethics code: the three from above plus
- fidelity and responsibility: keep trust with participants, take responsibility, no conflict of interest
- integrity: be accurate in practices

Institutions must have review board (IRB), at least 5 people
- one scientist, one person with primary concerns in non-scientific area, one community member

Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee
- Generally animal use is for when human use is very unethical
- Edward Taub & silver spring monkeys

Low-risk studies exempt, e.g. literature review, non-sensitive data
Informed consent

Construct validity:
	Translation validity: operationalization
		Face: it looks like what we want to measure
		Content: the measure contains all parts of the theory
	Criterion validity
		Predictive: predict future outcomes
		Concurrent: correlation with established measure of same construct
		Convergent: correlation with similar construct
		Discriminant: weak association with unrelated constructs

Inference validity:
- Internal
- External

Reliability:
- test-retest
- inter-rater reliability
- internal consistency: scores are similar across all items of a scale, measured using Cronbach's Alpha

Unreliable: affects validities

Reliable vs valid measures

Introduces noise:
- Respondent's carelessness
	- Motivate them, emphasize importance, pay them, design appealing survey, reverse score, avoid even scales
- Bad phrasing (video)
	- Avoid double negatives
- Order of questions: e.g. favorite fruits, kiwi or banana? how many times do you eat fruits? (first will impact last)
	- counter-balancing
- Social desirability
	- neutral questions, anonymous surveys, measure as covariate
- Self-serving bias
	- Indicate that there are no right or wrong answers, instruct participant to not think just react


Observational research
- avoids biases of survey data by directly observing behavior
- little info about cognition, limited causal claims
1. Naturalistic
2. Participant: set of predetermined conditions
	1. Observer bias (clearly operationalize variables, multiple observers, masked design)
	2. Observer effect: self-fulfilling prophecy (gifted students experiment; masked design, unobtrusive observation)
	3. Reactivity: people change behavior when other's are present (deception, habituation)
