**CRISPR** technology allows scientist to make changes to DNA in cells. Came about through a research project aimed at finding how bacteria fight viral infections. Discoverers receive Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2020.

It turns out some bacteria have an adaptive immune system called CRISPR that allows them to detect bacteriophage DNA and destroy it.

There are over $10^{31}$ phages in the world, with tens of millions of species. It is natural that bacteria evolved a defense mechanism.

**Cas9** is an enzyme part of CRISPR that seeks out cut and degrade viral DNA. IT can be harnessed as a genetic engineering technology, inserting or deleting bits of DNA into cells.

It has been used to change DNA in cells of mice and monkeys, as well as other organisms. Chinese scientists did it in human embryos. Philadelphia scientists used to remove DNA of integrated HIV virus from infected human cells.

##### How it works?

When viruses infect a cell, they inject their DNA. It allows that DNA to be plucked out of the virus, and inserted in little bits into the chromosome of the bacterium at a site called CRISPR. CRISPR stands for *clustered regularly interspaced short palindromic repeats*.

![[CRISPR-Cas9.png]]

Diamonds are repeats and squares are fragments of bacteriophage DNA.

It's a mechanism that allows cells to record the viruses they have been exposed to. *It is passed on to cells' progeny.*

The cell then transcribes RNA out of it. The RNA binds to a protein called Cas9 and functions like a sentinel in the cell. It searches through all of the DNA to find sites that match the sequences in the bound RNA. When they are found, the **complex binds to the DNA and allows Cas9 to cut up the viral DNA.**

![[CRISPR-Cas9-1.png]]

Cells can then **repair the double helix** by either pasting together the ends of broken DNA or it can repair the break by integrating a new piece of DNA at the cut's site.

Research is focusing on blood due to the easy distribution onto the cells.

##### Ethical Concerns

Right now we know nothing about genetic enhancement. Talents arise from thousands of different genes, few of which are known. Even with a straightforward characteristic such as height, genetic manipulation would be a tall order. Estimates point out that height could be influenced by as many as 93,000 genes.
