# Mental ability

A **mental ability** is a *cognitive* capacity measured by **maximal-performance** tasks — problems with correct answers, scored by how well a person performs *at their best* (reasoning, verbal, spatial, quantitative). It indexes capacity to process information and solve problems, not characteristic behavior. 

Two features define it:

- **Maximal, not typical, performance** — "best you can do," which separates it from personality traits, measured as *typical* performance,
- **Positive manifold** — mental abilities correlate positively with each other, producing the general factor [[g]].

It therefore excludes:

- **Motor skills** — physical, not cognitive; don't load on g.
- **Personality traits** — typical performance, not capacity.

The maximal/typical boundary is [[Lee Cronbach]]'s; the g/positive-manifold structure is [[Charles Spearman]]'s
